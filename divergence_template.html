Key takeaways
  • Mexico-Texas sits in structural surplus every Q3. Across all four years measured (2022 to 2025), it averages 1.0 to 1.1, the floor of the scale. This isn't a bad year, it's a permanent condition worth planning capacity around.
  • Eastern shortage regions have eased but haven't normalized. Indiana and Mid-Atlantic both ran near 4.8 in Q3 2022 (crisis territory) and have since dropped closer to their 4-year averages, though both still sit materially above adequate.
